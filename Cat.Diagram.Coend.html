Coends🔗

Let F:Cop×C→DF : \ca{C}\op \times \ca{C} \to \ca{D}F:Cop×C→D be a functor, which, by the general yoga of bifunctors we think of as combining a contravariant action F(−,x):Cop→DF(-,x) : \ca{C}\op \to \ca{D}F(−,x):Cop→D and a covariant action F(x,−):C→DF(x,-) : \ca{C} \to \ca{D}F(x,−):C→D of C\ca{C}C on D\ca{D}D1. As a concrete example, we could take C=BR\ca{C} = \bf{B}RC=BR, the 1-object Ab\AbAb-category associated to a ring RRR — then the functors F(−,x)F(-,x)F(−,x) and F(x,−)F(x,-)F(x,−) would be left- and right- RRR-modules, respectively. In fact, let us focus on this case and consider two modules AAA and BBB, incarnated as a pair of functors A:BRop→AbA : \bf{B}R\op \to \AbA:BRop→Ab and B:BR→AbB : \bf{B}R \to \AbB:BR→Ab.

In this situation, we may study the tensor product (of modules!) A⊗RBA \otimes_R BA⊗R​B as being an “universal object where the actions of AAA and BBB agree.” In particular, it’s known that we can compute the tensor product of modules as being (the object in) a coequaliser diagram like

A⊗R⊗B⇉A⊗B↠A⊗RB, A \otimes R \otimes B \tto A \otimes B \epi A \otimes_R B\text{,} A⊗R⊗B⇉A⊗B↠A⊗R​B,

where the undecorated A⊗BA \otimes BA⊗B stands for the tensor product of abelian groups, and the two maps are given by the RRR-actions of AAA and BBB. More explicitly, letting a⊗ba \otimes ba⊗b stand for a tensor, the equation a⊗rb=ar⊗ba\otimes rb = ar\otimes ba⊗rb=ar⊗b holds in A⊗RBA \otimes_R BA⊗R​B, and the tensor product is the universal object where this is forced to hold.

Going back to the absurd generality of a bifunctor F:Cop×C→DF : \ca{C}\op \times \ca{C} \to \ca{D}F:Cop×C→D, we may still wish to consider these sorts of “universal quotients where a pair of actions are forced to agree,” even if our codomain category D\ca{D}D does not have a well-behaved calculus of tensor products. As a motivating example, the computation of left Kan extensions as certain colimits has this form!

We call such an object a coend of the functor FFF, and denote it by ∫cF(c,c)\int^c F(c,c)∫cF(c,c) (or ∫F\int F∫F for short). Being a type of colimit, coends are initial objects in a particular category, but we will unpack the definition here and talk about cowedges instead.

Formalisation🔗

record Cowedge (F : Functor (C ^op ×Cat C) D) : Type (coend-level F) where

A cowedge under a functor FFF is given by an object w:Dw : \ca{D}w:D (which we refer to as the nadir, because we’re pretentious), together with a family of maps ψc:F(c,c)→w\psi_c : F(c,c) \to wψc​:F(c,c)→w.

  field
    nadir : D.Ob
    ψ     : ∀ c → D.Hom (F.₀ (c , c)) nadir

This family of maps must satisfy a condition called extranaturality, expressing that the diagram below commutes. As the name implies, this is like the naturality condition for a natural transformation, but extra! In particular, it copes with the functor being covariant in one argument and contravariant in the other.

    extranatural
      : ∀ {c c′} (f : C.Hom c c′)
      → ψ c D.∘ F.first f ≡ ψ c′ D.∘ F.second f

A coend, then, is a universal cowedge. In particular, we say that (w,ψ)(w,\psi)(w,ψ) is a coend for FFF such that, given any other wedge (w′,ψ)(w',\psi)(w′,ψ) under FFF, we can factor www through w′w'w′ by a unique map e:w→w′e : w \to w'e:w→w′. Furthermore, the map eee must commute with the maps ψ\psiψ, meaning explicitly that eψa=ψa′e\psi_a = \psi'_aeψa​=ψa′​.

record Coend (F : Functor (C ^op ×Cat C) D) : Type (coend-level F) where

  field
    cowedge : Cowedge F

Unfolding the requirement of uniqueness for eee, we require that, given any other map f:w→w′f : w \to w'f:w→w′ s.t. fψa=ψa′f\psi_a = \psi'_afψa​=ψa′​ for all objects aaa, then e=fe = fe=f. This is exactly analogous to the uniqueness properties of colimiting maps for every other colimit.

  field
    factor   : ∀ (W : Cowedge F) → D.Hom cowedge.nadir (W .nadir)
    commutes : ∀ {W : Cowedge F} {a} → factor W D.∘ cowedge.ψ a ≡ W .ψ a
    unique
      : ∀ {W : Cowedge F} {g : D.Hom cowedge.nadir (W .nadir)}
      → (∀ {a} → g D.∘ cowedge.ψ a ≡ W .ψ a)
      → g ≡ factor W
