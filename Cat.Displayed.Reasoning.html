Reasoning in displayed categoriesüîó

Contrary to the reasoning combinators for precategories, reasoning in a displayed category is much harder. The core of the problem is that the type Displayed.Hom[_] of displayed morphisms is dependent, so all but the most trivial paths over it will similarly be dependent paths. We prefer to work instead with non-dependent paths and substitution, using the from-pathp and to-pathp combinators to adjust to the situation.

A fundamental operator is moving a morphism between displayed hom‚Å°\homhom-spaces depending on a path in the base category, so we provide a shorthand syntax for that here. You can think of this as being an abbreviation for subst because‚Ä¶ that‚Äôs what it is.

hom[_] : ‚àÄ {a b x y} {f g : B.Hom a b} ‚Üí f ‚â° g ‚Üí E.Hom[ f ] x y ‚Üí E.Hom[ g ] x y
hom[ p ] f‚Ä≤ = subst (Œª h ‚Üí E.Hom[ h ] _ _) p f‚Ä≤

hom[_]‚Åª : ‚àÄ {a b x y} {f g : B.Hom a b} ‚Üí g ‚â° f ‚Üí E.Hom[ f ] x y ‚Üí E.Hom[ g ] x y
hom[ p ]‚Åª f‚Ä≤ = subst (Œª h ‚Üí E.Hom[ h ] _ _) (sym p) f‚Ä≤

Since equality in the base hom‚Å°b(‚àí,‚àí)\hom_b(-,-)homb‚Äã(‚àí,‚àí) is a proposition, we can always adjust the path we‚Äôre transporting over to get something more convenient.

hom[]-reindex
  : ‚àÄ {a b x y} {f g : B.Hom a b} (p q : f ‚â° g) {f‚Ä≤ : E.Hom[ f ] x y}
  ‚Üí hom[ p ] f‚Ä≤ ‚â° hom[ q ] f‚Ä≤
hom[]-reindex p q {f‚Ä≤} = ap (Œª e ‚Üí hom[ e ] f‚Ä≤) (B.Hom-set _ _ _ _ p q)

Next come the most important lemmas: Moving substitution in and out of composite morphisms. The hom[]-whisker-r combinator says that substituting on the right of a composition is the same thing as composing first, then adjusting by a path which leaves the ‚Äúleft‚Äù composite unchanged.

hom[]-‚àô
  : ‚àÄ {a b x y} {f g h : B.Hom a b} (p : f ‚â° g) (q : g ‚â° h)
      {f‚Ä≤ : E.Hom[ f ] x y}
  ‚Üí hom[ q ] (hom[ p ] f‚Ä≤) ‚â° hom[ p ‚àô q ] f‚Ä≤
hom[]-‚àô p q = sym (subst-‚àô (Œª h ‚Üí E.Hom[ h ] _ _) _ _ _)

To understand why these whiskering lemmas have such complicated types, recall that the ‚Äúdisplayed composition‚Äù operator has type

hom‚Å°f(b,c)√óhom‚Å°g(a,b)‚Üíhom‚Å°f‚àòg(a,c), \hom_f(b, c) \times \hom_g(a, b) \to \hom_{f \circ g}(a, c)\text{,} homf‚Äã(b,c)√óhomg‚Äã(a,b)‚Üíhomf‚àòg‚Äã(a,c),

so if we have some path p:g=g‚Ä≤p : g = g'p:g=g‚Ä≤, the composite f‚àòp‚àógf \circ p_*gf‚àòp‚àó‚Äãg will have type hom‚Å°f‚àòg‚Ä≤(‚àí,‚àí)\hom_{f \circ g'}(-,-)homf‚àòg‚Ä≤‚Äã(‚àí,‚àí), but the composite f‚àògf \circ gf‚àòg has type hom‚Å°f‚àòg(‚àí,‚àí)\hom_{f \circ g}(-,-)homf‚àòg‚Äã(‚àí,‚àí). Hence the need to adjust that composite: we can‚Äôt just get rid of the transport p‚àó(‚àí)p^*(-)p‚àó(‚àí).

hom[]-whisker-r

  ‚Üí f‚Ä≤ E.‚àò‚Ä≤ hom[ p ] g‚Ä≤ ‚â° hom[ ap (f B.‚àò_) p ] (f‚Ä≤ E.‚àò‚Ä≤ g‚Ä≤)
hom[]-whisker-r {f = f} {a‚Ä≤ = a‚Ä≤} {_} {c‚Ä≤} {f‚Ä≤} {g‚Ä≤} p i =
  comp
    (Œª j ‚Üí E.Hom[ f B.‚àò p (i ‚à® j) ] a‚Ä≤ c‚Ä≤)
    (Œª { j (i = i0) ‚Üí f‚Ä≤ E.‚àò‚Ä≤ transport-filler (Œª i ‚Üí E.Hom[ p i ] _ _) g‚Ä≤ j
       ; j (i = i1) ‚Üí hom[ ap (f B.‚àò_) p ] (f‚Ä≤ E.‚àò‚Ä≤ g‚Ä≤)
       })
    (transport-filler (Œª i ‚Üí E.Hom[ f B.‚àò p i ] _ _) (f‚Ä≤ E.‚àò‚Ä≤ g‚Ä≤) i)

hom[]-whisker-l

  ‚Üí hom[ p ] f‚Ä≤ E.‚àò‚Ä≤ g‚Ä≤ ‚â° hom[ ap (B._‚àò g) p ] (f‚Ä≤ E.‚àò‚Ä≤ g‚Ä≤)

  comp

    (transport-filler (Œª i ‚Üí E.Hom[ p i B.‚àò g ] _ _) (f‚Ä≤ E.‚àò‚Ä≤ g‚Ä≤) i)

The rest of this module is made up of grueling applications of the three lemmas above:

hom[]-smashr

-- Idea: p is equal to some composite p‚Ä≤ ‚àô q, but it's mis-associated or
-- something. We combine the reindexing to fix the association and

hom[]-weave
  : ‚àÄ {a b} {a‚Ä≤ b‚Ä≤} {f g h : B.Hom a b} {h‚ÇÅ‚Ä≤ : E.Hom[ f ] a‚Ä≤ b‚Ä≤} {h‚ÇÇ‚Ä≤ : E.Hom[ g ] a‚Ä≤ b‚Ä≤}

  ‚Üí hom[ p ] h‚ÇÅ‚Ä≤ ‚â° hom[ q ] h‚ÇÇ‚Ä≤

    hom[]-reindex p (p‚Ä≤ ‚àô q)
